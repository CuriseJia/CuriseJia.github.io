abstract: Improving the radiation resistance of integrated circuits (ICs) by identifying sensitive areas on the device-under-test (DUT) that are susceptible to single-event-effects (SEE) is an effective way to prevent SEE hazards. In this study, we introduce OML, a framework to enhance high-resolution online trajectory detection using the HiBeam-SEE system. The aim is to accurately localize the position of beams impacting an IC in real time, consequently reducing the need for extensive offline data storage. To optimize the speed of deployment, we employ a reparameterization method to merge branches of the neural network’s backbone in the deployment scenario. Additionally, we integrate a newly designed irregular convolution, attention mechanism, and loss function to elevate the localization accuracy. OML demonstrates exceptional real-time processing capabilities, achieving a standard error (SE) of 1.83 um in handling data generated by the HiBeam-SEE system on real datasets. Specifically, when processing a single image containing 25+ laser or heavy-ion trajectories, our framework operates at an impressive 110+ fps on a single GPU.
